Our progress

Last time, we defined what collaborative learning was and tried to figure out what we could do within the field. By the end of our process, we ended up with 3 creative objectives:

  • In what ways might we engage cross-disciplinary teams to efficiently catalyze groundbreaking advancements?
  • In what ways might we foster a transformative learning environment that lets students simultaneously gain depth of their respective fields as well as breadth across disciplines?
  • In what ways might we empower students with learning disabilities to achieve two-way enrichment in their education?

Eventually, we chose to work on the last objective. Check out our process by clicking on the links below.

We want to make a meaningul and sustainable difference in the collaborative learning environments of students with learning disabilities.

Why did we choose this topic?

We discussed common interests and analyzed various industries in an attempt to find points for improvement. Among those mentioned, there was a general inclination to revolutionize the current problems of the education system by listing off multiple prevalent issues. From there, we innovated vision statements over and over again right before converging to the particular creative objective we have now.


The current scope of this group is limited to the experience of learning disabilities as it is a commonality that we have personally undergone. The ways in which this group will pursue such a creative objective remains to be tweaked, though it is of essence for us to keep ideating before taking any measurable and committed steps.


One thing is certain, however: our vocation will remain oriented towards those who experience a psychological or physical hindrance with their ability to effectively work in collaborative environments. We align ourselves with those who wish to be productive, but cannot. We understand their plight. To help them is our purpose.


The group intends to continue fine-tuning the ideation process until it could create a solid mission statement to abide by.

How did we conceptualize our creative objective?

It began as a mildly interesting topic to rant about, and slowly but surely it developed into a plan to make a difference. From a topic as generalized as “the learning process”, we played around with various ideas and pet peeves that came from a place of pain. We love learning, but there are things about it that make it difficult for everyone to enjoy learning. We kept asking ourselves “why?” and “for what purpose?” repeatedly until we found a creative objective that everyone felt sufficiently motivated to work with. There existed some iterations of the vision statement that felt impractical, so through word associations we managed to scrounge up a manifestation of a goal that felt crazy and open-ended yet interpretable enough to create smaller SMART sub-objectives with.

We enhanced collaboration and innovation by blending our diverse perspectives and experiences into a cohesive framework.

  • Our group revisited the foundational stages of our project, allowing us to rethink and refine our approach.
  • We recognized the need for both a broad, holistic view and a detailed, nuanced understanding of the project.
  • We united our diverse experiences under a common creative objective which eventually fostered a rich array of ideas and synthesis methods.
  • Our application of various concepts evolved into cohesive frameworks.
  • Our collaborative learning extended beyond theoretical concepts, thereby breaking free from repetitive patterns.

Our group transitioned from traditional teaching to enhance our IDEAS framework using a building block approach. This method considered dynamic factors and challenged our initial views on collaboration.
